There is a particular intimacy in studying a language not as a tool for conversation, but as an object of curiosity. You learn to hear its rhythms, trace its history in borrowed words, map the topology of its grammar — all without the social pressure of actually having to use it correctly with strangers.

Maltese is like this for me. I work with it daily, I can read it, parse it, run models over it. But ask me to order a coffee in Valletta and I would flounder.

This used to bother me. It felt like incomplete knowledge — a book read but never discussed. But I've come to think there's something valuable in the distance. You notice things a native speaker would never notice, precisely because you're always slightly outside the language, looking in.
